23. Identification and description ball


Jawaban:

A ball is a round object (usually spherical, but can sometimes be ovoid)[1] with various uses. It is used in ball games, where the play of the game follows the state of the ball as it is hit, kicked or thrown by players. Balls can also be used for simpler activities, such as catch or juggling. Balls made from hard-wearing materials are used in engineering applications to provide very low friction bearings, known as ball bearings. Black-powder weapons use stone and metal balls as projectiles.

Although many types of balls are today made from rubber, this form was unknown outside the Americas until after the voyages of Columbus. The Spanish were the first Europeans to see the bouncing rubber balls (although solid and not inflated) which were employed most notably in the Mesoamerican ballgame. Balls used in various sports in other parts of the world prior to Columbus were made from other materials such as animal bladders or skins, stuffed with various materials.

As balls are one of the most familiar spherical objects to humans, the word "ball" may be used to refer to or describe spherical or near-spherical objects.

"Ball" is used metaphorically sometimes to denote something spherical or spheroid, e.g., armadillos and human beings curl up into a ball, we make a ball with our fist.

26. Helen and Ivan had the same number of coins. Helen had a number of 50-cent coins and 64 20-cent coins. These coins had a mass of 1.134kg. Ivan had a number of 50-cents coins and 104 20-cent coins. A) given that each 50-cent coin is 2.7g more heavier than a 20-cent coin, what is the mass of Ivan's coin in kg?​


Jawab:

1.026 kg

Penjelasan dengan langkah-langkah:

Let n be the number of Helen's 50-cent coins.

It is given that:

Helen and Ivan had the same number of coinsHelen had a number of 50-cent coins and 64 20-cent coinsIvan had a number of 50-cents coins and 104 20-cent coins

104 – 64 = 40

From these facts, we know that Ivan had 40 20-cent coins more than Helen's. Therefore, we can get that the number of Ivan's 50-cent coins which equals to:

n – 40

Let x be the mass of each 50-cent coin, and y be the mass of each 20-cent coin.

At Helen's side, we can get that

nx + 64y = 1.134kg = (1.134 * 1000) g

⇔  nx + 64y = 1,134     .....(i) (in grams)

Given that each 50-cent coin is 2.7g heavier than a 20-cent coin, we can get that

x = y + 2.7      (in grams)

Substitute x to equation (i):

n(y + 2.7) + 64y = 1,134     .....(ii)

Let m be the mass of Ivan's coins, we can get that:

(n – 40)x + 104y = m

⇔  nx – 40x + 104y = m

Then we substitute x with y + 2.7.

⇔  n(y + 2.7) – 40(y + 2.7) + 104y = m

⇔  n(y + 2.7) – 40y – 40(2.7) + 104y = m

⇔  n(y + 2.7) + 64y – 108 = m

Substitute n(y + 2.7) + 64y with 1,134 we got in equation (ii):

⇔  1,134 – 108 = m

⇔  m = 1,026 g = (1,026 / 1,000) kg

⇔ m = 1.026 kg

CONCLUSION:

∴  The mass of Ivan's coins is 1.026 kg.
